Translational visual neuroscience

Exploring the Brain Through Vision.

We develop vision-based biomarkers and research tools — from retinal imaging to eye-tracking and digital visual testing — turning the eye into a window on neurodegeneration.

BegiBrainLab is the Visual Pathway Laboratory of the Neurodegenerative Diseases Group at Biobizkaia Health Research Institute.

Research focus

BegiBrainLab combines visual neuroscience, retinal and brain imaging, functional visual assessment, immersive technologies, open-access software and multimodal artificial intelligence to study neurodegeneration through the visual system.

Retina / MRI

Retinal and visual pathway neuroimaging

Structural and microvascular retinal imaging, together with structural and functional MRI, to characterise retinal neurodegeneration, visual brain networks and disease progression.

Function

Visual function and eye-tracking

Computerised psychophysics, oculomotor control, pupillometry, electroretinography and visual cognition as functional readouts of the visual pathway.

AR / MR

Augmented and mixed reality

Immersive, ecological and realistic environments to assess and train visual cognition, functional performance and activities of daily living in neurodegenerative diseases.

Open software

Open-access visual assessment software

Development of open-access digital tools for standardised visual assessment, psychophysical testing, eye-tracking workflows and reproducible visual neuroscience research.

Projects

Current and previous projects structure the lab around Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, retinal and brain imaging, functional visual assessment and digital technologies.

ISCIII-FIS · 2026–2028

VISUAL-ADPD

A multimodal study of visual impairment in Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease, integrating OCT/OCT-A, MRI, cognition, blood biomarkers and digital visual assessment.

Alzheimer’s disease

BegiAlz

A prospective Alzheimer’s cohort focused on retinal imaging, visual function and biomarkers in prodromal and early clinical stages.

Parkinson’s disease

BegiPark

A prospective Parkinson’s cohort enabling longitudinal assessment of visual, retinal, clinical and cognitive markers.

Digital tool

BegiBrainTool

An open-source computerised platform under development and validation for standardised assessment of visual function using psychophysics, eye-tracking, pupillometry and autonomic sensors.

AI / OCT

RetiMAT

An open tool for OCT image analysis developed in collaboration with Mondragon Unibertsitatea, supporting quantitative retinal research.

Mixed reality

ALZAIN / Alzhain

A proof-of-concept mixed-reality platform for functional assessment and stimulation in early Alzheimer’s disease through activities of daily living.
