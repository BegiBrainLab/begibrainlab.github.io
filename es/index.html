Neurociencia visual traslacional

Biomarcadores visuales para la salud cerebral.

BegiBrainLab estudia el sistema visual como una ventana accesible a la neurodegeneración, integrando imagen retiniana, RM cerebral, psicofísica, eye-tracking, sensores autonómicos e inteligencia artificial en enfermedad de Alzheimer y Parkinson.

Líneas de investigación

El laboratorio combina cohortes clínicas prospectivas, tecnología biomédica avanzada y análisis cuantitativo de datos para generar biomarcadores reproducibles, no invasivos y clínicamente escalables.

OCT / OCT-A

Neuroimagen retiniana

Structural and microvascular retinal imaging to characterise neurodegeneration and disease progression.

Function

Función visual y eye-tracking

Computerised psychophysics, oculomotor control, pupillometry and visual cognition as functional readouts of the visual pathway.

AI

Biomarcadores multimodales

Integration of retina, MRI, digital visual metrics, clinical data, neuropsychology and blood biomarkers to identify interpretable multimodal signatures.

Proyectos

Current and previous projects structure the lab around Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, retinal and brain imaging, functional visual assessment and digital technologies.

ISCIII-FIS · 2026–2028

VISUAL-ADPD

A multimodal study of visual impairment in Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s disease, integrating OCT/OCT-A, MRI, cognition, blood biomarkers and digital visual assessment.

Alzheimer’s disease

BegiAlz

A prospective Alzheimer’s cohort focused on retinal imaging, visual function and biomarkers in prodromal and early clinical stages.

Parkinson’s disease

BegiPark

A prospective Parkinson’s cohort enabling longitudinal assessment of visual, retinal, clinical and cognitive markers.

Digital tool

BegiBrainTool

An open-source computerised platform under development and validation for standardised assessment of visual function using psychophysics, eye-tracking, pupillometry and autonomic sensors.

AI / OCT

RetiMAT

An open tool for OCT image analysis developed in collaboration with Mondragon Unibertsitatea, supporting quantitative retinal research.

Mixed reality

ALZAIN / Alzhain

A proof-of-concept mixed-reality platform for functional assessment and stimulation in early Alzheimer’s disease through activities of daily living.
